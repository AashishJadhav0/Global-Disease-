Measles (Morbillivirus)

Description: Measles is a highly contagious viral disease caused by the Morbillivirus. It primarily affects children and can lead to serious health complications, including pneumonia, encephalitis, and death.

Symptoms

  • High fever
  • Cough
  • Runny nose
  • Red, watery eyes
  • Rash that usually starts on the face and spreads

History

Measles has been known since at least the 9th century. Widespread vaccination programs began in the 1960s, drastically reducing its prevalence in many parts of the world.

Present Condition

Despite the availability of a vaccine, outbreaks still occur due to vaccine hesitancy and lack of access in some regions. Surveillance and rapid response are critical to containment.

Future Outlook

Efforts continue worldwide to eliminate measles through vaccination and education. Research into better vaccine delivery methods is ongoing.

Prevention Tips

  • Get vaccinated with the MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) vaccine.
  • Maintain good hygiene and handwashing.
  • Avoid close contact with infected individuals.
  • Follow local health guidelines during outbreaks.
